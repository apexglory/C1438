Exercise Time, Study Time and Calories influence my Daily Feeling #Attempt 2

Intent

This project is about the relationship between exercise time, study time and calories and my daily feeling. I want to know if I have a good daily feeling, how much exercise time, study time and calories I need. I also want to know if I have a bad daily feeling, how much exercise time, study time and calories I need.

Assumptions

  • Moderate exercise time or calorie intake will make me feel good
  • Too little or too much exercise time or calorie intake will make me uncomfortable
  • Study time has little effect on feel
  • If the daily study and exercise time is too long for many consecutive days, it will have a long-term negative impact on feel

Data example

{ "date": "17/10/2022", "exercise time": 100, "learn time": 2, "calories": 1157, "feel": 2 }

Inspiration

I am a student and I have a lot of homework to do. I also want to have a good daily feeling. So I want to know how much exercise time, study time and calories I need to have a good daily feeling. This calendar chart is inspired by the calendar chart by Mike Bostock. I think this calendar chart is very powerful, by adding different elements such as color, area and even expression could present a very intuitive effect, and it can show the relationship between exercise time and study.

What differences between attempt 1 and attempt 2?

I believe that the calendar chart can fully prove my hypothesis, but in a1, I only use the color and the circle area to reflect the relationship between the two data. So in a2, I decided to continue to explore technology to better show the connection between the four sets of data. The specific improvements are as follows:

  1. Use the red line segment and the blue line to indicate the learning time and exercise time respectively, and their units are the same.
  2. Use the wave area chart to represent the calorie intake for the day
  3. Use a cute expression in the upper left corner to express the mood of the day, and put the date text in the upper right corner.

I don't think I have achieved the best effect yet. So I will continue to improve it later.

What & Why?

The question I want to explore is the link between exercise time, study time, calorie intake and feelings of the day over longer time scales. Therefore, compared with scatter plots or other graphics, using a calendar chart can better reflect the trend of multidimensional data on the linear x-axis

Initial conclusion

If I study or exercise for a long time for many consecutive days, I will not feel very good if I take a full rest in time for the next few days.
